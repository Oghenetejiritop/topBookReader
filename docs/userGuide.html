Introduction

Welcome to the TOP Book Reader user guide. An E-Book reader for Microsoft windows that has an easy-going navigation, user-friendly interface and offers accessibility to all book lovers.

Features

Here are the basic features offered by the program: It currently supports over five E-book formats which includes:

  • Word Document (.docx)
  • Electronic Publisher (.epub)
  • Portible Document Format (.pdf)
  • HyperTest Markup Language (.html|.htm)
  • Text File (.txt)
  • Rich Text Format(.rtf)
      •     Additional features include:
* Mini dictionary (An offline built-in english dictionary for quick search of words).
* Text To Speech service (To electronically read your book aloud.)
    Please note: In the future releases; support for other book formats and unique functionalities will be introduced.

      Accessibility Support
      
    It is currently compatible with the following screen readers but not limited to:
* Non-Visual Desktop Access NVDA

Usage

This section will take you through the usage of TOP Book Reader and show you various ways to navigate and use the program.


How to Open a Document

To open a document; on the menu bar, head to the file menu and click on the "Open a file..." item; or clickl the open icon on the tool bar.
Short cuts: "ctrl+o".

Page Navigation

To either go to the previous or next page on an opened document, you can click on the next or previous button on the main window.
Short cuts: "n" for next page while "p" for previous page.

Read Aloud

Whenever you want your book's content to be read by an electronical voice; click on the play button to read aloud and the pause button to stop reading.
Short cuts: "alt+p" for both resume and pause reading aloud.

Access Recently Opened Documents

To find recent documents opened before; click on the recent icon on the main window.
Short cuts: "alt+r".

Text To Speech Adjustment Setting

Click the Voice Setting icon on the right-hand part of the main window to select your preference.
Short cuts: "alt+v".

Exiting The Program

To exit the  program; on the menu bar, head to the file menu and click on the "exit program..." item; or clickl the exit icon on the tool bar.
Short cuts: "alt+f4".

Add To Bookmarks

Click the Bookmark icon on the tool bar to add the highlighted text to the bookmarks.
Short cuts: "ctrl+b".

Bookmarks History

Click the Bookmark History icon on the tool bar to view all the bookmarks of the currently opened document.
Short cuts: "ctrl+shift+b".

Navigate To Page

If you will like to navigate to a specific page; head to the Pages  /Bookmarks menu on the menu bar.
Then, click the Go To Page item.
Short cuts: "ctrl+g".

Table Of Content Of Opened Document

Like to view the table of contents of an opened document; you can head to the Pages  /Bookmarks menu on the menu bar. Then, click on the Table Of Content item.
Short cuts: "ctrl+shift+t".

Zooming In And Out 

If you will like to zoom in or out on the text; navigate to the View menu. Then, click on the Zoom In or Zoom Out item.
Short cuts: "alt+i" to zoom in and "alt+o" to zoom out.


Font Adjustment


If you prefer a different font on the display content component, click on the adjust font icon on the tool bar; or head to the View menu and click on the Adjust Font item.
Short cuts: "ctrl+f".


Accessing The Dictionary


Whenever you like to check the meaning of a vocabulary , you can either click on the dictionary icon on the tool bar or head to the advance menu and click on the Access Dictionary Meaning menu item.
Short cuts: "ctrl+d".




Recommended Sites For Downloads


Whenever you like to explore sites where you can get meaningful contents; head to the advance menu and click on the Download More Books From Here menu item. This will open the Recommended Sites dialog box.
Short cuts: "ctrl+r".


Accessing The Dictionary


Whenever you like to check the meaning of a vocabulary , you can either click on the dictionary icon on the tool bar or head to the advance menu and click on the Access Dictionary Meaning menu item.
Short cuts: "ctrl+d".
